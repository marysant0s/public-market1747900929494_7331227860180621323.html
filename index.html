Public Markets in Rural Philippines

By Maria L. Santos | Published 2021

Abstract

Public markets remain essential to the economic and social fabric of rural municipalities in the Philippines. This study investigates attendance trends and highlights findings from Binalbagan and Caramoan, where 2–4% of residents are active public market-goers.

Introduction

Public markets or palengkes are crucial in Philippine rural communities. This article explores their role in commerce and social life amidst changing consumer behavior.

Methodology

This study uses census data and municipal reports from Binalbagan and Caramoan, along with interviews conducted in 2021.

Key Findings

Only 2–4% of residents are daily or frequent market-goers:

  • Binalbagan (Population: 71,469): 1,429–2,859 individuals
  • Caramoan (Population: 51,728): 1,035–2,069 individuals

Factors Affecting Engagement

  • Geographic Accessibility
  • Household Self-Sufficiency
  • Alternative Retail Sources
  • Market Infrastructure

Discussion

Despite low daily engagement, markets impact employment and food access. Weekly market events still draw larger crowds.

Conclusion

Public markets continue to play a key role in rural economies. Investment in infrastructure and modernization is recommended.
